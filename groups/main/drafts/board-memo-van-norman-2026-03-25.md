## Request

I am requesting 10 minutes on the March 25 agenda for an informational presentation by **Charlie Van Norman**, an ed tech specialist with deep experience in K-12 math and AI-assisted learning platforms.

## Background

RCSD has made meaningful progress on student achievement — our December 2025 Dashboard results showed ELA scores up 11.1 points and math up 8.6 points — but equity gaps persist, particularly for homeless students and African American students. The Board has invested in several instructional tools and programs over the past two years, including:

- **i-Ready** diagnostic assessments (K-8 reading and math, reviewed Oct 2025 with longitudinal data back to Fall 2023)
- **Imagine Learning Illustrative Mathematics** curriculum (grades 6-8, adopted Mar 2025)
- **Benchmark 2022** ELA curriculum pilot (Taft and Garfield, approved May 2025)
- **California Reading Literacy Project** teacher training (presented Jan 2026)
- **RCSD Cohesion Plan** for instructional alignment, common assessments, and targeted reading PD for grades 3-5 (Sep 2025)
- **College Advising Prep** math tutoring at Kennedy and McKinley ($240K, Sep 2025)
- **Multitudes** dyslexia screening for K-2 (approved May 2025)

The Board also heard a presentation on AI in education in November 2025, covering how teachers are using AI for lesson plans, rubrics, differentiated instruction, and IEP drafting.

The question before us now is whether AI-powered adaptive learning platforms have matured to the point where they can meaningfully accelerate student progress in literacy and numeracy — particularly for students who are behind grade level.

## About the Presenter

**Charlie Van Norman** is an ed tech specialist whose career has focused on making math accessible and engaging for K-12 students:

- Created **Super Math World**, reaching over 100,000 students
- Founded **MathBreakers.com**, a 3D puzzle-based math platform for grades 5-8
- Served as **Founding CTO of StarCoach AI**, building AI-assisted K-12 math assessment tools

## Proposed Presentation Content

Mr. Van Norman will present a practical framework for evaluating and deploying AI-assisted learning tools in a district like RCSD. His recommended four-stage approach:

1. **Research** — Survey what other California districts have implemented, what worked, and how their experience maps to RCSD's student population and existing programs.

2. **Discovery** — Work with RCSD teachers to validate the approach, select vendors, and design a pilot. Mr. Van Norman emphasizes that success hinges on teacher buy-in and ownership throughout the process.

3. **Pilot** — A structured 10-12 week pilot with pre-agreed success metrics and single-threaded ownership for day-to-day execution.

4. **Rollout** — Scope and timeline to be determined based on pilot results.
